Jyotirlinga Pradosh Special 1008 Shiv Gayatri Mantra Jaap, Pradosh-Stotrashtakam and Pradosh Vrat Katha For Resolving Conflicts in Relationships

The Pradosh Vrat is considered extremely auspicious and is believed to bestow the blessings of Lord Shiva in the Kali Yuga. This sacred festival occurs twice a month on the Trayodashi Tithi, and on this day, observing a fast (vrat) with utmost devotion and worshipping Lord Shiva can lead to happiness, prosperity, and the attainment of wealth in life. It is said that the significance of this vrat was first explained by Lord Shiva to Mata Sati, according to which any devotee who observes Pradosh Vrat can get the blessings of marital happiness from Lord Shiva. According to scriptures, the worship of the twelve Jyotirlingas is considered most significant for the worship of Lord Shiva. It is said that worshipping the Jyotirlingas leads to the attainment of eternal merit (akshay punya) for individuals. Among them, the Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga in Madhya Pradesh is believed to be where Lord Shiva daily plays the game of dice with Mata Parvati. Hence, devotees believe that worshipping this Jyotirlinga can bestow happiness and peace in marital life. The scriptures explain many powerful mantras of Lord Shiva, including the Shiva Gayatri Mantra. Reciting this mantra grants mental peace and positive energy to individuals. On the day of Pradosh, chanting this mantra, reciting the Pradosh Stotrashtakam and Pradosh Vrat Katha can give the complete benefits of this fast. Therefore, participate in the 1008 Shiv Gayatri Mantra Jaap, Pradosh-Stotrashtakam and Pradosh Vrat Katha at the Shri Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga Temple in Madhya Pradesh through Sri Mandir and receive the blessings of Lord Shiva and Maa Parvati.

Shri Brihaspati Temple, Kashi, Uttar Pradesh

The fourth Jyotirlinga among the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva is Shri Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga, considered a Swayambhu lingam. It is located in the Khandwa district of Madhya Pradesh on the island of Mandhata or Shivpuri in the Narmada River. Here, the Jyotirlinga exists in two forms. One is known as Mamleshwar and the other as Omkareshwar. Mamleshwar is located a little distance away on the southern bank of the Narmada from Omkareshwar. Despite being separate, they are counted as one. The pronunciation of 'Omkar' first emerged from the mouth of the creator Brahma. The recitation of the Vedas also begins with 'Om'. It is believed that the Narmada river itself flows here in the shape of 'Om'. According to scriptures, merely visiting Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga can liberate one from all sins. The glory of the Omkareshwar Kshetra is mentioned in the Skanda Purana, Shiva Purana, and Vayu Purana.

According to a mythological story, Lord Shiva comes here to rest at night after wandering through all the three worlds. It is said that this is the only temple on earth where Shiva and Parvati play the game of dice every night. After the Shayan Aarti at night, a dice board is set up here, and the Garbhgriha is closed. Surprisingly, even though no one can enter the temple at night, the dice are found scattered every day. This fact further enhances the religious significance of this temple. This is why after visiting all other pilgrimages, a visit and worship at Omkareshwar holds special importance. Pilgrims bring water from all other holy places to offer at Omkareshwar, only then are all pilgrimages considered complete; otherwise, they are considered incomplete.
